Effective codeing Practice!

29 November 2020

There are many different aspects of being productive. Your keyboard and pointing device position and comfort, your screen distance and posture in your chair, setting up your editor and knowing frequently used key shortcuts (jump between left/editors and tabs, opening and closing files, commenting out lines of code), your tools and tool chain (all in one IDE or jump back and forth to command line), access to debugging and performance tools, scripting anything that you do manually, continuous integration, committing small changes frequently so you always have a clean slate (don’t have to worry about trying new code and having to remember to undo it), write down or diagram complicated logic (if you don’t really understand the problem, it is difficult to code), get enough sleep, take breaks, don’t multitask too much, make sure you have some block of uninterrupted time, write your code in layers, building from simple, reusable functions to large applications. Keep each layer understandable and simple. Good luck!
